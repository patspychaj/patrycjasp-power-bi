Design philosophy

Three principles
behind every screen

01
Clarity before beauty
Every decoration that doesn't serve comprehension is noise. I start by stripping layouts to the essential, then add visual richness only where it aids perception — not to impress.
02
Color as language
Colors carry meaning. Red means alert, green means good, accent color means "look here first." I define a semantic palette at the start of each project and enforce it without exception.
03
Designed for decisions
A dashboard succeeds when a user can open it and act within 30 seconds. I design information flows to support decision-making paths, not just display data.

Sales Intelligence
Command Center
A real-time executive dashboard for a retail group operating across 8 cities. Designed specifically for war-room reviews — full dark theme, high contrast KPIs, and a visual hierarchy that gets to the point in under 10 seconds.
Design decisions
🌑
Dark theme chosen for focus, not fashion Used in a dedicated screen room. Dark reduces ambient glare and keeps attention on numbers.
🎯
Single accent color = one call to action Orange is reserved exclusively for the metric that requires attention — never used decoratively.
📐
3-column KPI strip at the top Eye-tracking research shows users scan top-left first. Critical metrics are placed in F-pattern reading order.

Design process

From messy data
to clear decisions

A repeatable design system applied to every engagement — so quality is consistent, not accidental.

1
Discovery
Who reads this? What decision does it support? What do they do in the next 5 minutes after opening it?
2
Information arch.
Grouping metrics by decision type. Establishing visual hierarchy before touching Power BI.
3
Color system
Defining semantic palette: accent, alert, neutral, positive. Rules before pixels.
4
Prototype
Figma wireframe tested with 2–3 actual users before building in Power BI. No wasted dev time.
5
Deliver & refine
Handover with a design spec doc. 2 weeks of supported refinement post-launch.
